This page is all about my hobbies (some which you may find boring) !


1.KPOP

I've always enjoyed listening to all sorts of K-Pop since the start of my GCSEs. It was introduced to me by my cousin (who is an avid BTS fan), and I do appreciate the fact that it's not everybody's cup of tea, and that's absoolutely fine !!

I mostly listen to BTS, but I do listen to other popular groups such as BlackPink, Twice, Red Velvet and Enhypen.

2.ART

In my spare time (actually, when I have spare time), I like to engage in anything artistic, whether that be doodling out of boredom or any DIY projects.

Prior to A Levels, I used to to a lot of digital art for fun using Krita drawing software. I never had any idea what I was going to fully draw, I would just go with the flow, which was quite relaxing !

One of my favourite art forms has to be origami. I really like origami because I think that it looks really authentic and elegant (until your parents tell you to throw it out because it takes too much space).

3.BAKING

If I had to choose one dessert to live off for the rest of my life, it would be cheescake. Whenever I have an urge to bake, I always go for this, because not only is it easy to make, it is sooo good !

If I do bake, it would be because I am bored, so I may make e.g. cupcakes or cookies (and have my little sister tell me they taste bad). I would also have my mum complain about the mess I have made, but that's ok because it was fun !

4.PROGRAMMING

Now, this might sound silly because most people think writing code is a boring and painful process. Well, yes, sometimes. However, the sense of achievement knowing that you have developed a piece of code/ software for other people to use is unreal, and the fact that you've done it all by yourself makes you even more proud.

Now, the topic of favourite programming languages can be a sensitive one, especially when different languages are for different uses. All in all, I think that my favourite programming language is Python. This is because it is the very first language I have learnt, and through it I was able to understand key programming concepts very easily. The syntax is a lot neater compared to other languages (cough, Java) and so it is easy for beginners.

5.GAMING

When it comes to gaming, I only do it for fun and I am not competitive. I like any sort of web, mobile and video games, and I don't have a specific favourite genre - just a game that is unique to any other games that I have played.

My favourite game would have to be Stardew Valley. I love this game because it does not get repetitive, unlike most video games. The challenges within the game are also quite fun such as the mines (I hate those ghosts), and you can interact with NPCs and build relationships with them.

I also don't mind Fortnite as it's fun with friends, but it tends to get boring (all we play is Battle Royale).

6.ANIME

I'm not crazy about anime, but I like to dip in and out of it when I wish to. I like animes based on sci-fi/horror/magical themes, as I think they are interesting.

Here is a list of animes I have watched:

  • Death Note
  • !Haikyu
  • Jujutsu Kaisen
  • Neon Genesis Evangelion
  • Black Clover (most of it)
  • NGE: The End of Evangelion
  • The first two episodes of My Hero Academia (did not like it)

My favourite anime out of the ones I have watched has to be Death Note. This is because it's completely different to the ones that I have seen so far and I like the storyline behind it. The storyline is interesting because it basically discovers whether it is right or wrong to use death as a means to remove all the bad things from the world, which is quite an ethical and moral question.
